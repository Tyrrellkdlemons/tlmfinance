# TLM Finance — Pitch Voiceover Script

**Purpose:** Record this in one take over the slide deck (Loom, QuickTime, OBS). Total length ≈ 12–15 minutes at a natural pace. Slide numbers match `TLM-Finance-Pitch-Today.pptx`.

> Tip: read the **bold** lines a beat slower. They land harder.

---

## Slide 1 — Title (~25s)

Good morning. I'm **Tyrrell Lemons**, and I'm here to show you a tool I built called **TLM Finance** — at *tlmfinance dot netlify dot app*. It's free, it's private, and it's mobile-first. It's not officially affiliated with The Last Mile yet — but it's voice-and-tone matched to TLM, it's built for the same population TLM serves, and **it's ready for partnership today**. The next 12 to 15 minutes are about: what it does, who it's for, how it sits next to thelastmile.org, and what I'm asking you for at the end.

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem (~35s)

Three numbers, all from the homepage of *thelastmile dot org* as of today. The U.S. prison population is up **seven hundred percent** since the 1970s. **Sixty-one percent** of people released come back within ten years. But TLM graduates? **Under five percent.** That gap is the proof that what TLM does works.

Now here's the thing: TLM has cracked the inside-the-walls half — the coding bootcamps, the entrepreneurship program, the audio production. But the day someone walks out, they're walking into the most financially unsupported moment of their life. No bank account. No ID. No money app built for the first 90 days. **That's the gap I'm trying to close.**

---

## Slide 3 — Why I Built This (~30s)

A quick word on why this exists at all. I have family who came home. I watched what the first ninety days looks like — and every money app on the market was built for someone who already had a job, an ID, and a checking account. None of them were built for the window where you don't yet.

So the brief became one sentence: **"the last mile of school, the first mile of freedom."** That's the design philosophy of every screen you're about to see.

---

## Slide 4 — Three Doors In (~35s)

Three doors into one mission. **First** — the *Freedom Plan Panel*. It's a bottom sheet that pops up on every page. Tap the eight things you need first — money, ID, job, housing, education, transportation, health, family — and the site builds a printable 72-hour starter list.

**Second** — the *Customizable Plan*. Seven steps, fifty smart suggestion chips so the user almost never has to type, four quick-start templates for common scenarios, and a live plan-health score from zero to a hundred.

**Third** — the *My Plan view*. Read-only summary you can print, email, or hand to a case manager.

Now let me walk you through each one.

---

## Slide 5 — Where This Fits (~30s)

Side-by-side. On the left — what TLM does today: thirteen facilities, eight states, sixteen hundred alumni, seventy-five percent employment, under five percent recidivism. That's the inside-the-walls work.

On the right — what *tlmfinance dot netlify dot app* adds: a money planner, document checklist, AI helper, vetted resources for **after** release. Privacy-first, mobile-first, works offline.

Same population, same mission, complementary surfaces. **I'm not trying to compete with TLM. I'm trying to extend it.**

---

## Slide 6 — Site Architecture (~25s)

The site is twelve user-facing pages plus a hidden admin panel and the presentation hub you're looking at the deck inside right now. Home, Planner, My Plan, Learn, Hub, Watch, Feed, Radio, Media — and three compliance pages: Privacy, Terms, and Data-deletion, required by Google and Facebook OAuth.

**Twelve pages, one coherent journey** from "I just got out" to "here's my 90-day plan."

---

## Slide 7 — Walkthrough 1: FPP (~35s)

Walk-through one — the **Freedom Plan Panel**. Tap "Generate planner" on any page and this slides up from the bottom of the screen. Eight cards: money plan, ID and documents, job path, housing, education, transportation, health and meds, family. Tap the ones that apply.

Press "Generate my 72-hour plan" and the site builds a printable list — replace the state ID at the DMV, replace the Social Security card at *ssa.gov*, open a checking account or activate a prepaid card, save 988 and the SAMHSA helpline. Real, specific, ordered.

And here's the move: **those tasks carry over automatically to the deeper Customizable Plan.** No re-typing. The starter is the seed of the bigger plan.

---

## Slide 8 — Walkthrough 2: Steps (~30s)

Walk-through two — the **Customizable Plan**. Seven steps. *Income. Benefits. Expenses. Debts. Goals. Documents.* And a *Sequence* card for the first 72 hours, 30 days, 60, and 90.

Each step is a finished thought. None of them depend on perfectly remembering what was on the previous one. The whole flow takes **about seven minutes** end-to-end. And if the user signs in — totally optional — the whole plan syncs to the cloud so they can pick it up on any device.

---

## Slide 9 — Chips & Templates (~30s)

Two design choices that matter more than they sound. **One** — about fifty smart suggestion chips across the planner. Tap "wages, job" to add an income line. Tap "rent" to add an expense. Tap "phone bill." Tap "transit." The user almost never has to type.

**Two** — four quick-start templates for common scenarios: *just released with no income yet; working part-time with gaps; school plus side hustle; or family support, living with a relative.* Pick one and the planner pre-fills with realistic placeholder rows that the user then edits.

**Nobody stares at a blank screen.**

---

## Slide 10 — KPIs + Plan Health (~25s)

Live KPIs update on every keystroke. Monthly income on top-left. Monthly expenses top-right. **Net cash flow** — green when positive, red when negative. Savings goal at ten percent of income.

And the centerpiece: the **Plan Health Score**. Zero to a hundred, composite of how complete the plan is, the cash-flow ratio, and how many documents the user has collected. Eighty-four out of a hundred — strong start. **The score doesn't shame anyone; it tells them what to fill in next.**

## Slide 11 — Marcus Intro (~25s)

Now let me make it concrete. Meet **Marcus** — he's not real, but every detail is composited from real reentry profiles. Marcus is thirty-four. He's six days from release. Four years served. He has a brother who can host him two weeks. A state ID that expired in 2022. No bank account, no smartphone yet. And a job interview he can probably get, if he can show up clean and on time.

The next four slides walk through how Marcus uses TLM Finance from day minus six to day ninety.

He taps Generate planner. He picks five cards — money, ID, job path, housing, health and meds. **In two minutes he has a printable starter list.** He prints it on the center's printer.

Plan health: thirty-two out of a hundred. Red.

**And that's fine.** The score is a guide, not a grade. The moment something real lands — a job offer, a benefit confirmation — it goes up.

**From red to green.** Day zero: thirty-two. Day seven: forty-one. Day thirty: fifty-five — first calendar bill cycle. Day sixty: seventy-two — net cash flow is positive. **Day ninety: eighty-six.** Documents at eleven of twelve. Ninety-day sequence eleven of fourteen done.

## Slide 16 — Privacy & Auth (~25s)

Privacy and auth — because this matters for *this* population more than any other. **No analytics. No third-party fonts.** The plan saves to your browser's local storage on every keystroke.

Sign-in is OPT-IN — only required if you want to sync across devices. Three providers: email plus Google plus Facebook OAuth through Supabase. **Row-level security** on every database table — only the row owner can read or write their own plan. Period.

## Slide 18 — Chatbot (~30s)

There's a floating AI helper in the bottom right of every page. **Thirteen free LLMs in rotation** server-side: Llama-3.3-70B, DeepSeek V3 and R1, Qwen-2.5-72B, QwQ, Gemini 2.0 Flash, Hermes-3-405B, Nemotron-70B, plus four smaller models. Five fallback endpoints. **No API key in the browser, ever.**

Topic guards built in: it stays on money, banking, credit, AI, careers. It DECLINES legal advice — parole, probation, expungement, sentencing, immigration — and redirects to the National Reentry Resource Center, Root and Rebound, 211, and 988 for crisis.

Chat history lives in memory only. **Never persisted. Never logged.**

## Slide 19 — Admin Panel (~25s)

There's an owner panel that's intentionally hidden. Three discreet entry methods: click the logo seven times in three seconds, type the literal string *T-L-M-admin* anywhere, or enter the Konami code. **Any entry path then hits a hashed-password gate** — SHA-256 with a per-install salt — with a five-strikes-and-five-minute lockout.

Inside, the admin can edit hero copy, resources, videos, people, see sign-ins and audit logs, toggle individual pages on or off, and deploy.

One Netlify function — the chatbot relay. **WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility audit applied.** Reduced motion. Dark and light themes.

## Recording tips

- **Tool:** Loom (free) or QuickTime → File → New Screen Recording → record entire screen with audio. OBS works too.
- **Pace:** ~150–160 words per minute is a comfortable presentation rhythm. Don't rush slides 11–15 (the Marcus arc) — those land best slow.
- **Pauses:** breathe between sections. The line breaks here are where I'd pause.
- **Read in passes:** record the whole thing once flat. If a section sounds wobbly, re-record JUST that slide and stitch in editing.
- **No video of you needed:** voiceover over the deck is the standard, plenty.

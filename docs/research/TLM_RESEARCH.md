## Mission & Origin

The Last Mile is an Oakland, CA-based 501(c)(3) nonprofit founded in **2010** by tech entrepreneurs **Chris Redlitz** and **Beverly Parenti** at **San Quentin State Prison**. Originally a business and entrepreneurship incubator, TLM evolved into one of the first in-prison coding programs in the United States.

The mission centers on providing opportunities for personal and professional growth for justice-impacted individuals through education and technology training, with the conviction that **a job is the key to successful reentry**.

## Programs

1. **In-Prison Education** — Full-scale tech labs inside correctional facilities with secure internet and live remote instruction. Full-time program split into two 6-month segments.
2. **Workforce Reentry** — Modules to equip returning citizens with employment-readiness skills (internships, apprenticeships, freelance). Continued education paired with job placement.
3. **PATH Framework (2025+)** — Project management, sales, and entrepreneurship in addition to web development.
4. **Curriculum tools (2025+)** — Google Workspace simulator, AI literacy modules.

Sources:
- https://thelastmile.org/programs/
- https://thelastmile.org/programs/reentry/

## Impact Statistics (cite exact numbers, do not mix vintages)

> Always show the **source year** next to the metric on the website.

**From the 2024 Annual Report (published Mar 2025):**
- Served **451 students** across **16 facilities**.
- Average daily enrollment: **310 participants**.
- Programs in **8 states, 16 facilities, 21 classrooms** in 2024.
- **75%** employment rate (alumni out 6+ months).

**Site/blog references (2025–2026):**
- Programs operating in **16 facilities across 7 states** (CA, IN, OK, MI, ND, MT, MA) per the 2026 outlook page.
- Nearly **1,000 alumni** have returned home with skills and ongoing support.
- **72%** of alumni out 6+ months are employed.
- Reported recidivism: below **8%** (compared with US national average ~64%).
- **90%** of alumni pursue further education.

**Major employers of graduates** (per "The Last Mile in 2026"): Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Apple, Meta, Nvidia, NASA, Capital One.

## Project Rebound (CSU Fullerton)

CSUF Project Rebound supports the higher education and successful reintegration of formerly incarcerated students. Established at CSUF in **2016**; has supported **300+** formerly incarcerated students.

**Services:**
- Scholarships, academic resources, peer mentoring, tutoring.
- Academic and financial counseling, career placement.
- **John Irwin Memorial House** — reentry housing with wraparound support.
- Harm-reduction workshops, restorative justice circles, gender-specific groups.

**Eligibility:** Anyone impacted by the criminal justice system at any level.

Sources:
- https://www.fullerton.edu/rebound/
- https://www.fullerton.edu/rebound/about-us/
- https://www.fullerton.edu/rebound/services/john-irwin-house.html
- https://www.calstate.edu/csu-system/news/Pages/Project-Rebound.aspx

## Financial Planning Frameworks (used in our planner)

### CFPB — *Your Money, Your Goals: Focus on Reentry*
Companion guide for working with returning citizens. Covers SMART goals, criminal background screening rights, banking access, debt, and identity documents. Use as the conceptual backbone of the planner.

Source: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/consumer-tools/educator-tools/your-money-your-goals/companion-guides/

### FDIC — *Money Smart for Adults*
14-module instructor-led financial-education curriculum. Available in multiple languages, accessible for people with disabilities, and shown to influence financial behavior in research.

Source: https://www.fdic.gov/consumer-resource-center/money-smart-adults
